PRONE STATE

ACTIVATION

*   During the **[Deployment Phase](/Deployment_Phase "Deployment Phase")**, players may deploy one or more of their Troopers Prone by placing a Prone Token beside them.
*   A Trooper may change its status from Normal to Prone, and vice versa, at **either** the start of their Movement **or** at the end of their Movement, by performing any [Short Skill](/Short_Skill "Short Skill") or [ARO](/ARO "ARO") with the [Movement Label](/Movement_Label "Movement Label") (except for [Jump](/Jump "Jump")) _[(See FAQs.)](#FAQs)_ or as a result of a failed [Guts Roll](/Guts_Roll "Guts Roll"). However, this will affect their [Silhouette](/Silhouette "Silhouette") and the distance they can move around the game table.
*   If a Trooper enters **[Unconscious State](/Unconscious_State "Unconscious State")**, it automatically enters Prone State unless it has the [Motorcycle](/Motorcycle "Motorcycle") piece of Equipment, or it is a [VH](/VH "VH") or [TAG](/TAG "TAG"), Troop Types that cannot enter Prone State.

EFFECTS

*   Prone Troopers have a [Silhouette](/Silhouette "Silhouette") (S) value of X, that is, equivalent to their base's height and width.
*   While in this state, Troopers halve the distance they can move when performing any Skill with [Movement Label](/Movement_Label "Movement Label") ([Move](/Move "Move"), [Dodge](/Dodge "Dodge")...), or when moving as the result of a failed [Guts Roll](/Guts_Roll "Guts Roll").
*   This state does not affect Automatic Special Skills or Automatic Equipment, except when explicitly stated in their description.

CANCELLATION

*   A Trooper may change its status from Normal to Prone, and vice versa, at **either** the start of their Movement **or** at the end of their Movement, by performing any [Short Skill](/Short_Skill "Short Skill") or [ARO](/ARO "ARO") with the [Movement Label](/Movement_Label "Movement Label") (except for [Jump](/Jump "Jump")) _[(See FAQs.)](#FAQs)_ or as a result of a failed [Guts Roll](/Guts_Roll "Guts Roll"). However, this will affect their [Silhouette](/Silhouette "Silhouette") and the distance they can move around the game table.
*   This state is automatically canceled when the [Jump](/Jump "Jump") Common Skill is declared.
*   Canceling a Trooper's **[Unconscious State](/Unconscious_State "Unconscious State")** automatically cancels their Prone State as well.

  

SEE ALSO

**[General Movement Rules](/General_Movement_Rules "General Movement Rules")**

*   When performing a Short Skill or ARO **with the [Movement Label](/Movement_Label "Movement Label")** (except for Jump), or when moving after failing a [Guts Roll](/Guts_Roll "Guts Roll"), a Trooper may enter or leave Prone State at **either** the start of their Movement **or** the end of their Movement, at no cost.  
    However, if they move in Prone State, this will reduce the distance they can move.

Restrictions Chart

[TAGs](/TAG "TAG") cannot go [Prone](/Prone "Prone") or declare [Cautious Movement](/Cautious_Movement "Cautious Movement").

Troopers mounted on [Motorcycles](/Motorcycle "Motorcycle") cannot go [Prone](/Prone "Prone"), declare [Cautious Movement](/Cautious_Movement "Cautious Movement"), [Climb](/Climb "Climb"), use [ladders](/Ladders "Ladders"), or perform an upwards [Jump](/Jump "Jump").

Troopers with the [Aerial](/Aerial "Aerial") Special Skill cannot go [Prone](/Prone "Prone") or declare [Cautious Movement](/Cautious_Movement "Cautious Movement").

Vehicles ([VH](/VH "VH")) cannot go [Prone](/Prone "Prone") or declare [Cautious Movement](/Cautious_Movement "Cautious Movement").

  

FAQs
----

N5 FAQ

Version: 0.0.0, Oct 2025

**Q: Which Skills and AROs allow a Trooper to enter or cancel Prone State?**

A: A Trooper can enter or cancel Prone State when performing any Skill or ARO with the Movement Label (except Jump and Berserk).

_Related Pages: [General Movement Rules](/General_Movement_Rules "General Movement Rules"), [Labels](/Labels "Labels"), Prone State_

  

N5 FAQ

Version: 0.0.0, Oct 2025

**Q: If a Trooper enters Prone State at the end of their move, how does this affect the distance they can move?**

A: It doesn’t. They move normally and then finish the end of the move prone.
