*   When moving Troopers around the battlefield, players must measure the complete route (including, for example, any detour to avoid obstacles) and must always use the same part of the base for their measurements.
*   [MOV](/MOV "MOV") values indicate the maximum distance the unit can move, but there is no minimum. Troopers do not have to exhaust their movement when they declare [Move](/Move "Move").
*   A Trooper can vault over, onto, or off any scenery item whose height is equal to or lower than the height of the Trooper's [Silhouette Template](/Silhouette_Template "Silhouette Template"), with no need to declare [Climb](/Climb "Climb") or [Jump](/Jump "Jump") (see [vaulting example](#Moving_and_Obstacles)).
*   This vertical movement is not taken into account when measuring how far the Trooper moves, but the Trooper counts as moving up and over the obstacle for the purposes of [LoF](/LoF "LoF").
*   Likewise, while they Move, a Trooper may peek over the edge of a roof or other surface with up to half of its Silhouette, notably to have LoF to Targets located on the same level or on a different level (see the example and Sibylla explains).
*   A Trooper can move through [Allied](/Allied "Allied") or [Neutral](/Neutral "Neutral") Models, Markers, and Tokens, but they cannot end their movement in the space occupied by another Model, Marker, or Token.
*   Troopers have a [LoF](/LoF "LoF") arc of 360˚ while they are moving.
*   After Moving one of your models, you can leave it facing any direction.
*   A Trooper's Movement ends automatically whenever he enters [Silhouette contact](/Silhouette_contact "Silhouette contact") with an enemy, even if the specified movement route is cut short as a result.
*   In order to pass between an Enemy Trooper and an obstacle without entering Silhouette contact, the space in between must be wider than your Trooper's base.
*   When performing a Short Skill or ARO **with the [Movement Label](/Movement_Label "Movement Label")** (except for [Jump](/Jump "Jump")) _[(See FAQs.)](#FAQs)_, or when moving after failing a [Guts Roll](/Guts_Roll "Guts Roll"), a Trooper may enter or leave [Prone State](/Prone_State "Prone State") at **either** the start of their Movement **or** the end of their Movement, at no cost.  
    However, if they move in Prone State, this will reduce the distance they can move.
*   Independently of the type of movement performed (Move, Jump, Climb, etc.), the movement cannot, in any way, cause the Trooper to fall. If the Trooper cannot reach a new location with their Move, Jump, Climb... they do not perform the movement and they perform an [Idle](/Idle "Idle") instead.
*   Once declared, Troopers always reach the end of their Movement, even if they enter a [Null](/Null "Null") or [Immobilized](/Immobilized "Immobilized") State due to successful enemy Attacks along the way.

Movement and Obstacles

Example of vaulting over obstacles lower than [Silhouette](/Silhouette "Silhouette") height.

![eng-vault-2.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/eng-vault-2.jpg) Conversely, in the second picture, the Trooper may not pass over the obstacle without declaring [Jump](/Jump "Jump") or [Climb](/Climb "Climb"), since the obstacle is higher than the height of the Trooper’s [Silhouette Template](/Silhouette_Template "Silhouette Template") on one side.

Vaulting On or Off Surfaces

During a single movement, a Trooper can vault up or down multiple levels that are each equal to or lower than the height of its [Silhouette Template](/Silhouette_Template "Silhouette Template"), even if their combined height is higher than its Silhouette Template. However, there must be a surface at least half the size of its base between each of those levels.

Movement Sequence and Final Facing

Sibylla's Advice

When moving, only half of the Trooper's base is required to be in contact with the surface they are moving on. Therefore, when moving, a Trooper can move part of its base over the edge of the surface, for instance to gain [LoF](/LoF "LoF") to Targets located on the same level or on a different level. To make movement and interactions during the game easier, scenery elements such as parapets and railings must be considered when drawing LoF (see example).

REMEMBER

Once they declare movement, Troopers always reach the end of their Movement, even if they enter a [Null](/Null "Null") or [Immobilized](/Immobilized "Immobilized") State due to successful enemy Attacks along the way.

  

Example
-------

Example of Movement

![eng-general-movement-example-1.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/eng-general-movement-example-1.jpg) In his Active Turn, the Fennec is leaning against a wall in [Total Cover](/Total_Cover "Total Cover"). He does not have [LoF](/LoF "LoF") to the Senku, on the other side of the building. The Fennec declares his first Short Skill: [Move](/Move "Move").

Therefore, the Player will measure the trajectory’s distance, determine his direction and path, then move the Fennec’s base so that his base peeks around the corner to gain LoF to his target while still in [Partial Cover](/Partial_Cover "Partial Cover").

Once he has seen the Senku, the Fennec’s model returns to his original position in Total Cover. His Move declaration will take him to the corner and back.

Since the Senku also gains LoF to the Fennec, he declares his [ARO](/ARO "ARO"): [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack").  
The Fennec then declares the second Short Skill of his Order, another BS Attack.  
In the subsequent [Face to Face Roll](/Face_to_Face_Roll "Face to Face Roll"), the Senku must apply the Partial Cover MODs.

![eng-general-movement-example-2.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/eng-general-movement-example-2.jpg) However, he wins the Face to Face Roll, while the Fennec fails his [Saving Roll](/Saving_Roll "Saving Roll"). Then, the Fennec would end his Order in [Unconscious State](/Unconscious_State "Unconscious State") behind Total Cover, where he finished his movement.

FAQs
----

N5 FAQ

Version: 0.0.0, Oct 2025

**Q: Which Skills and AROs allow a Trooper to enter or cancel Prone State?**

A: A Trooper can enter or cancel Prone State when performing any Skill or ARO with the Movement Label (except Jump and Berserk).

_Related Pages: General Movement Rules, [Labels](/Labels "Labels"), [Prone State](/Prone_State "Prone State")_

  

N5 FAQ

Version: 0.0.0, Oct 2025

**Q: If a Trooper enters Prone State at the end of their move, how does this affect the distance they can move?**

A: It doesn’t. They move normally and then finish the end of the move prone.

_Related Pages: General Movement Rules, [Moving and Measuring](/Moving_and_Measuring "Moving and Measuring"), [Prone State](/Prone_State "Prone State")_

  

N5 FAQ

Version: 0.0.0, Oct 2025

**Q: Can you vault while Climbing or Jumping?**

A: Yes. Climb and Jump follow the General Movement Rules, including vaulting.

_Related Pages: [Climb](/Climb "Climb"), [Climbing Plus](/Climbing_Plus "Climbing Plus"), General Movement Rules, [Jump](/Jump "Jump"), [Super-Jump](/Super-Jump "Super-Jump")_

  

N5 FAQ

Version: 0.0.0, Oct 2025

**Q: Can anything be placed on or end a move on Deployable Cover?**

A: No.

_Related Pages: [Deployable Cover](/Deployable_Cover "Deployable Cover"), General Movement Rules_
