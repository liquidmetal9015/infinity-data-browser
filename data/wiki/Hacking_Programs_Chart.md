Key to the Hacking Programs Quick Reference Chart
-------------------------------------------------

Each Hacking Program grants a series of MODs and advantages when used, that are reflected in charts listing the following information:

*   **Attack MOD.** A MOD that is applied to the user's [WIP](/WIP "WIP") Attribute.
*   **Opponent's MOD.** A MOD that is applied to an [enemy](/Enemy "Enemy") Trooper's [Attribute](/Attribute "Attribute") when performing a [Face to Face Roll](/Face_to_Face_Roll "Face to Face Roll").
*   **[Possibility of Survival (PS)](/Possibility_of_Survival_\(PS\) "Possibility of Survival (PS)").** The value used to determine the destructive capability of a successful [Hacking Program](/Hacking_Program "Hacking Program") Roll. Unless otherwise stated, **the [Attribute](/Attribute "Attribute") used in the [Saving Roll](/Saving_Roll "Saving Roll") to resist the effects of a Hacking Program is [BTS](/BTS "BTS")**.
*   **[Burst](/Burst "Burst") (B).** The number of dice the Active Player must roll when declaring the Hacking Program. When the B value is higher than 1, it may be concentrated on a single target or be divided among several targets. Remember that in the Reactive Turn the B value is generally 1, unless modified by a rule or Skill.
*   **Target.** The [Troop Type](/Troop_Type "Troop Type") that can be targeted by the [Hacking Program](/Hacking_Program "Hacking Program").
*   **Type of Skill.** Indicates the type of Skill ( [Long Skill, Short Skill, ARO](/Skills_and_Equipment_Module#Common_and_Special_Skills_in_Infinity "Skills and Equipment Module"), etc.) that must be spent to use the [Hacking Program](/Hacking_Program "Hacking Program").
*   **Special.** This indicates the special effects that the [Hacking Program](/Hacking_Program "Hacking Program")'s user may apply. This section may also indicate a [State](/State "State") that will be imposed on the target, or the type of [Ammunition](/Ammunition "Ammunition") and [Traits](/Traits "Traits") that may apply.

  

REMEMBER

Unless otherwise stated, the range of every Program is always the [Hacker](/Hacker "Hacker")'s [Hacking Area](/Hacking_Area "Hacking Area").

  

SEE ALSO

**[Quantronic Combat (Hacking)](/Quantronic_Combat_\(Hacking\)#Hacking_Devices_and_Hacking_Programs:_Characteristics "Quantronic Combat (Hacking)")**  
[Hacking Devices](/Hacking_Device "Hacking Device") and [Hacking Programs](/Hacking_Program "Hacking Program") have the following characteristics:

*   They act in the user's [Hacking Area](/Hacking_Area "Hacking Area").
*   They do not require [LoF](/LoF "LoF") to act, unless the Hacking Program's own description states otherwise.
*   Only Troopers in [Model](/Model "Model") form may be targeted by Hacking Programs.
*   They benefit, if applicable, from the target's [Targeted](/Targeted "Targeted") State.
