[BS Weapons](/BS_Weapon "BS Weapon") and [Equipment](/Equipment "Equipment") that use a Template have an Area of Effect that affects not only the main target, but also an area of the game table determined **by the size of the Template**.

The **Main Target** of a Template Weapon or Equipment is a Game Element that must be able to be designated as a valid target for the [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack"), from which the MODs applied to the Roll are determined, if a Roll is required. This Roll will be opposed separately, one by one, by the rest of the Enemies affected by the template.

_October 2025: Minor text changes in N5.2._

  
There are two types of Weapons and Equipment Templates:

*   [Direct Template](/Direct_Template_Weapons "Direct Template Weapons").
*   [Impact Template](/Impact_Template_Weapons "Impact Template Weapons").

  

IMPORTANT

If the Main Target is outside the [Area of Effect](/Area_of_Effect "Area of Effect") of the Template Weapon or Equipment, the [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack") is automatically considered a failure and no Game Element will be affected by the Template.

Types of Templates
------------------

In this rulebook, there are three kinds of Templates used by [Weapons](/Weapons "Weapons") and pieces of [Equipment](/Equipment "Equipment"): the Circular Template, the Large Teardrop Template and the Small Teardrop Template.

The height of the Templates used by Template Weapons and Equipment is equal to their radius, or half their width (except for those Ammunitions whose [Traits](/Traits "Traits") otherwise specify it).

![dual-types-of-templates-1024.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/dual-types-of-templates-1024.jpg)

  

Area of Effect
--------------

The Area of Effect of a Template is the area it covers with a single declaration of use. For example, if you declare an Attack using a Template Weapon, all Troopers or targets in [Silhouette contact](/Silhouette_contact "Silhouette contact") or inside the Area of Effect of the Template are affected by the Attack.

### Area of Effect of Template Weapons and Equipment

Area of Effect of Template Weapons and Equipment

EFFECTS

*   When using a Template Weapon or Equipment, any Trooper whose base or [Silhouette](/Silhouette "Silhouette") Template is in contact with the Template, or is partially or fully inside it, is affected by the Weapon or Equipment.
*   Any Trooper affected by a Template Weapon or Equipment can declare **[Dodge](/Dodge "Dodge")** as their second Short Skill or ARO, **even if they do not have [LoF](/LoF "LoF")** to the Trooper declaring the Attack.
*   As a general rule, **Dodging** an Attack with a Template requires a successful **[PH](/PH "PH") Roll**.
*   As an exception to this, in the following cases the Dodge requires **a PH -3 Roll** to avoid an Attack with a Template Weapon:
    *   If the Trooper affected by the Template Weapon Attack has no [LoF](/LoF "LoF") to the attacker.
    *   If the Template Weapon Attack is from a **[Deployable Weapon](/Deployable_Weapon "Deployable Weapon")** (a [Mine](/Mines "Mines"), for example).
*   Once placed, the Template remains on the game table until the [Resolution](/Resolution "Resolution") step of the Order.
*   The Area of Effect of a Template can affect [Allied](/Allied "Allied") Troopers as long as the Template has no [PS value](/Possibility_of_Survival_\(PS\) "Possibility of Survival (PS)") and does not inflict any [State](/State "State").
*   When several Troopers are activated at the same time (a [Coordinated Order](/Coordinated_Order "Coordinated Order") or [Fireteam](/Fireteams_Module "Fireteams Module"), for example), the Template affects every Trooper that was in [Silhouette contact](/Silhouette_contact "Silhouette contact") with its Area of Effect at any time during that Order, since everything happens simultaneously during the Order.
*   [Critical](/Critical "Critical") Hits with Template Weapons:
    *   When a Critical is rolled when using a Template Weapon, it counts as a Critical against the Main Target of the attack, and only the Main Target. Against any Secondary Targets affected by the Template, it is a successful Roll instead of a Critical.
*   Responding to a Template Weapon Roll:
    *   Against an Attack with a Template Weapon that required a Roll to hit whether it was an Impact Template Weapon or an [Intuitive Attack](/Intuitive_Attack "Intuitive Attack"), etc., each affected enemy may make a separate [Face to Face Roll](/Face_to_Face_Roll "Face to Face Roll") against the attacker.

  

IMPORTANT

Troopers affected by a Template Weapon do not benefit from the +3 MOD to the [Saving Roll (SR)](/Saving_Roll_\(SR\) "Saving Roll (SR)") for [Partial Cover](/Partial_Cover "Partial Cover").

  

SEE ALSO

**[Area of Effect of Special Terrain Zones](/Terrain_and_Scenery_Structures#Special_Terrain "Terrain and Scenery Structures")**  

In Infinity, the Area of Effect of a Special Terrain zone is the area in where its special effects are applied. Any Trooper in [Silhouette contact](/Silhouette_contact "Silhouette contact") with a Special Terrain zone, or whose base or [Silhouette Template](/Silhouette_Template "Silhouette Template") is covered at least partially by a Special Terrain zone, is equally affected by the Effects of the zone.

  

Template Weapons, and Allied and Neutral Troopers
-------------------------------------------------

Declaring Attacks against [Allied](/Allied "Allied") or [Neutral](/Neutral "Neutral") Troopers is not allowed, nor is it allowed to perform Attacks that may affect them, unless the Template lacks a [PS Attribute](/Possibility_of_Survival_\(PS\) "Possibility of Survival (PS)") value and does not inflict [States](/States "States"). Therefore, if when declaring an Attack with a Template Weapon, an Allied or Neutral Trooper would be affected by the Template, that shot is cancelled (but not other shots of that same [Burst](/Burst "Burst") that have no Allies or Neutrals within their Area of Effect). Any reactive Troopers within the Area of Effect of the cancelled shot can still declare [AROs](/ARO "ARO").

In this case of a cancelled shot, if a Weapon with the [Disposable](/Disposable "Disposable") Trait was used, the initially declared use is still consumed.

Troopers 2 and 3 may declare [AROs](/ARO "ARO") normally.

  

Template Weapons Into Close Combat
----------------------------------

Template Weapons placed on a group of Troopers engaged in [Close Combat](/Close_Combat "Close Combat") will always affect every Trooper involved, even if, due to the Template’s placement, it contacts only some of them. Players must take this into account, since Attacks cannot be performed against [Allied](/Allied "Allied") Troopers.

Template Weapons, Scenery Elements and Total Cover
--------------------------------------------------

A Template’s Area of Effect may be determined by the presence of scenery elements on the game board, as seen in the graphics.

In Infinity, different Templates have a Blast Focus, which is used to determine if a Trooper is affected by the Template, or if the Template’s Area of Effect has been blocked by [Total Cover](/Total_Cover "Total Cover") from intervening scenery elements. For a Trooper to be affected by the Template, it must be possible to trace a [LoF](/LoF "LoF") from the Template’s Blast Focus to the Trooper, without being blocked by [Total Cover](/Total_Cover "Total Cover").

The Blast Focus is the center of the Circular Template and the narrow end of the Teardrop Templates.

LoF from the Blast Focus to a Trooper may only be drawn within the Area of Effect of the Template itself. _[(See FAQs.)](#FAQs)_

  

Direct Template: Area of Effect, Blast Focus, and Scenery

![dual-templates-direct-scenery.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/dual-templates-direct-scenery.jpg)

The Scenery element completely covers the enemy Trooper. Therefore, [LoF](/LoF "LoF") cannot be traced from the Blast Focus so the Trooper is not affected by the Area of Effect. _[(See FAQs.)](#FAQs)_
