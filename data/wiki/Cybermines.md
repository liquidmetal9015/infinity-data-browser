Mines
-----

Mines are a type of Deployable Weapon used to control small areas of the battlefield and influence the movement of enemy troops. There are various types of Mines available, all of them work following the same mechanics, but they feature different types of Special Ammunition with their corresponding specific properties.

MINES

EFFECTS

*   When the player declares the [Place Deployable](/Place_Deployable "Place Deployable") Common Skill, instead of placing a Token to represent the Mine, he will place a [Camouflaged](/Camouflaged "Camouflaged") Marker CAMO(-3).
*   Replace the Camouflage Marker \[CAMO (-3)\] with a Mine Token \[MINE (-3)\] when it is [discovered](/Discover "Discover") by an enemy.
*   The Camouflage Marker and the Mine Token both benefit from the MODs provided by the [Mimetism](/Mimetism "Mimetism") (-3) Special Skill.
*   Mines have a 360˚ [LoF](/LoF "LoF") arc.
*   As a [Direct Template Weapon](/Direct_Template_Weapon "Direct Template Weapon"), when any type of Mine is triggered, a Small Teardrop Template must be placed and the PS and Ammunition specified in the Weapons Chart must be applied.
*   The Small Teardrop Template must be placed so that it affects the enemy Model or Marker that triggered the Mine.
*   **Restriction**: A Mine never triggers if the Small Teardrop Template would affect an [ally](/Allied "Allied"), even if that ally is [Unconscious](/Unconscious "Unconscious").
*   Once on the game table, Mines must trigger when an enemy Model or Marker declares or executes a Skill or [ARO](/ARO "ARO") inside their Trigger Area, checking it at that moment by placing the Small Teardrop Template. If it is determined that the Model or Marker is not within the Trigger Area, the Mine will neither **detonate nor be revealed**.
*   Once a Mine triggers, it is removed from play.
*   A Mine is a Template Weapon and a [Deployable Weapon](/Deployable_Weapon "Deployable Weapon"), so it imposes a -3 PH MOD to any attempt to [Dodge](/Dodge "Dodge") its effects.
*   The Trigger Area of a Mine (whether it is a Camouflage Marker or a Mine Token) is the area within the radius of the Small Teardrop Template, extended out from the edge of the base of the Mine.
*   The Trigger Area excludes any areas in [Total Cover](/Total_Cover "Total Cover") from the Blast Focus of the Small Teardrop Template (see graphic).
*   **Restriction**: A Mine cannot be placed if there is an enemy Camouflage Marker inside its Trigger Area. This restriction does not apply if there is a valid, non-camouflaged enemy inside the Trigger Area, or if an Intuitive Attack was declared.

Cybermines
----------

![cybermine0.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/cybermine0.jpg)

*   **CYBERMINES:**
    *   As Cybermines are Deployable Template Weapons that make [Comms Attacks](/Comms_Attack "Comms Attack"), they can only be evaded with a [Reset](/Reset "Reset"), rather than a Dodge, applying a -3 WIP MOD.
    *   Troopers who are hit by a Cybermine must make **two** Saving Rolls against [BTS](/BTS "BTS"), with PS 5. If the target fails either [Saving Roll](/Saving_Roll "Saving Roll"), it enters **[Stunned](/Stunned "Stunned")** State.
    *   However, any Troopers with the **[Hackable](/Hackable "Hackable") Characteristic ([HI](/HI "HI"), [REM](/REM "REM"), [TAG](/TAG "TAG"), [VH](/VH "VH")...)** and **[Hackers](/Hackers "Hackers")** who fail either Saving Roll enter **[Immobilized-B](/Immobilized-B "Immobilized-B")** State instead of Stunned State.

  

REMEMBER

[Dodge](/Dodge "Dodge") movement and movement from failed [Guts Rolls](/Guts_Rolls "Guts Rolls") does not generate [AROs](/ARO "ARO") or trigger [Deployable Weapons or Equipment](/Deployable_Weapon "Deployable Weapon").
