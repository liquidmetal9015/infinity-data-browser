Example 1: Creating a Fireteam
------------------------------

Case 1: Trooper Selection and Deployment

During the [Deployment Phase](/Deployment_Phase "Deployment Phase"), a White Banner player wants to build a Zhanshi Fireteam Core. As shown in the [Fireteams Charts](/Fireteams_Chart "Fireteams Chart"), in order to create a Zhanshi Fireteam Core it is mandatory to include at least 1 Zhanshi. The other members of the Fireteam can be any possible combination of the listed Troopers, as long as their maximum value is not exceeded. For example, 3 Zhanshi (Paramedic, Missile Launcher, and MULTI Sniper Rifle), 1 Zhanshi Yīshēng and Chief Cheong-Hee Jeong.

![eng-fireteam-examples-1v2.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/eng-fireteam-examples-1v2.jpg)

After deploying their Troopers, the player selects the Zhanshi (Paramedic) as Fireteam Leader, placing the Fireteam Core Token next to them. At the same time, the player tells their opponent which Troopers are part of the Fireteam and checks that all of them fulfil the [Coherency](/Coherency "Coherency") rule with respect to their Fireteam Leader. One of them is outside the [ZoC](/ZoC "ZoC") of the Fireteam Leader, but as it is still the first part of the player’s Deployment Phase the player places them inside the ZoC.

  

![sibylla-n5.png](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/sibylla-n5.png)

Sibylla's Advice

During the match, the player may create another Fireteam by spending a [Command Token](/Command_Token "Command Token"). In this case, they must be sure that all the Troopers that they want to be part of the Fireteam are within the [Zone of Control](/Zone_of_Control "Zone of Control") of the Trooper chosen as Fireteam Leader. Troopers that are not in [Coherency](/Coherency "Coherency") cannot become part of the Fireteam.

  

Case 2: Wildcards and Availability higher than 1

Kempeitai Troopers have [Availability](/Availability "Availability") 3 in the JSA Ōban [Sectorial Army](/Army_List#Sectorial_Army_Lists "Army List"). As shown in the [Fireteams Chart](/Fireteams_Chart "Fireteams Chart") image, as Kempeitai Troopers are Wildcards, they can be part of any Ōban Fireteam.

![eng-fireteam-examples-oban.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/eng-fireteam-examples-oban.jpg)

In the Wildcard Table, the Maximum of 3 indicates that the player may include up to 3 Kempeitai in each of the Sectorial’s Fireteams. Therefore, a Keisotsu Fireteam Core could be made up of 2 Keisotsu and 3 Kempeitai.

In the same way, you can include 2 Kempeitai in a Keisotsu Fireteam Core and 1 Kempeitai in a Domaru Fireteam Haris, because you cannot exceed the Sectorial’s Availability of 3 for the Kempeitai.

  

Example 2: Creating a Fireteam. Fireteam Level Bonuses
------------------------------------------------------

Creating a Fireteam. Fireteam Level Bonuses

Let us now analyze the Morat Fireteams. The following image shows the Morat [Fireteam Charts](/Fireteams_Chart "Fireteams Chart") and the [Fireteam Level Bonuses](/Fireteam_Bonuses "Fireteam Bonuses").

![eng-firetams-chart-morat-plain.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/eng-firetams-chart-morat-plain.jpg)

Fireteam Bonuses Chart

Fireteam Level

Bonus

Level 1

Fireteam members are activated with a single [Regular Order](/Regular_Order "Regular Order")

Level 2

[BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack") (+1 SD) Skill

Level 3

+3 [Discover](/Discover "Discover") and +1 [Dodge](/Dodge "Dodge")

Level 4

+1 [BS](/BS "BS")

Level 5

[Sixth Sense](/Sixth_Sense "Sixth Sense")

  
In this case, the mandatory minimum to build a Morat Fireteam is that at least one of the Troopers that has a (\*) in the Min column is part of the Fireteam. The rest of the Troopers can be any combination from the Table that does not exceed the allowed maximum. For example:

**Case 1: Fireteam Core**

*   3x Kurgat (Morat).
*   1x Yaogat (Morat).
*   1x Kaitok (Morat).

The Fireteam Level is determined based on the number of Troopers of the same Unit in a Fireteam. Since all members have '(Morat)' after their name, they gain all the Fireteam Level Bonuses.

In summary, all the Troopers with the same name, or which are labeled in the same way in their Fireteams Chart, may add the following Bonuses to any other MODs any of them might have on their Unit Profile:

*   [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack") (+1 SD) Skill.
*   +3 MOD to [Discover](/Discover "Discover") Rolls and +1 MOD to [Dodge](/Dodge "Dodge") Rolls.
*   +1 MOD to [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack") Rolls.
*   The [Sixth Sense](/Sixth_Sense "Sixth Sense") Special Skill.

  
**Case 2: Fireteam Core**

*   3x Morat Vanguard (Morat)
*   1x Kyosot
*   1x Zabuk

Another example of a five-member Fireteam Core. In this case, only 3 members of the Fireteam are Troopers belonging to the same Unit - those identified as (Morat). Therefore, all the Fireteam members have the Level 3 Bonuses.

In summary, all the members of such a Fireteam get the following Bonuses:

*   [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack") (+1 SD) Skill.
*   +3 MOD to [Discover](/Discover "Discover") Rolls and +1 MOD to [Dodge](/Dodge "Dodge") Rolls.
*   +1 MOD to [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack") Rolls.

  

Example 3: Order Expenditure Sequence and AROs (Fireteam in the Active Turn)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Order Expenditure Sequence and AROs (Fireteam in the Active Turn)

1.  The Active Player spends a [Regular Order](/Regular_Order "Regular Order") and places the Fireteam Leader Token besides the Trooper that they nominate as Fireteam Leader during that Order. Then they declare the first [Basic Short Skill](/Basic_Short_Skill "Basic Short Skill") of the Order. Next, they perform a [Coherency Check](/Coherency_Check "Coherency Check") to find out how many members are in Coherency with the Fireteam Leader, remaining within their Leader's [ZoC](/ZoC "ZoC").
2.  The Reactive Player declares the [AROs](/ARO "ARO") of all their Troopers who can react to any Fireteam member. Each enemy Trooper gets only one ARO against the whole Fireteam.
3.  The Active Player declares the second Short Skill of the Order.
4.  The Reactive Player declares the AROs of any Troopers who gain an ARO with the second Short Skill of the Order.
5.  Both players determine what [Modifiers](/Modifiers "Modifiers") (MODs) to apply, and the Active Player applies the [Fireteam Level Bonuses](/Fireteam_Bonuses "Fireteam Bonuses"). Both players make any required Rolls.
6.  At the end of the Order, during its [Conclusion](/Conclusion "Conclusion") and after having performed any movement due to [Guts Rolls](/Guts_Rolls "Guts Rolls"), the Active Player performs a Coherency Check to see if any member has left the Fireteam, or if the Fireteam has been cancelled.

  

Example 4: Move and Dodge (Fireteam in the Active Turn)
-------------------------------------------------------

Example 4: Move end Dodge (Fireteam in the Active Turn)

In the situation shown in the image, during their Active Turn, the Keisotsu Fireteam has declared [Move](/Move "Move") as the first Short Skill of the Fireteam Order. The Fireteam grants a single [ARO](/ARO "ARO") to each enemy, with each one declaring a [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack") ARO.

![eng-fireteam-examples-2.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/eng-fireteam-examples-2.jpg)

In this situation, the Fireteam Leader doesn’t have [LoF](/LoF "LoF") to enemy B, so they may only make a BS Attack [Face to Face Roll](/Face_to_Face_Roll "Face to Face Roll") against enemy A. Now the player has to decide if the Fireteam Leader declares BS Attack against enemy A, allowing enemy B to make a [Normal](/Normal "Normal") BS Attack Roll against Keisotsu 3. Or, alternatively, to declare [Dodge](/Dodge "Dodge") (a Skill with the [Movement Label](/Movement_Label "Movement Label")), so that all the Fireteam members can Dodge. This would result in two Face to Face [PH](/PH "PH") Rolls (one for the Fireteam Leader and another one for Keisotsu 3) against the enemies’ BS Attack. The other Keisotsu make Normal PH Rolls.

REMEMBER

During a [Fireteam Order](/Fireteam_Order "Fireteam Order"), the Skills that are performed by both the Fireteam Leader and the rest of the members are:

*   [Reset](/Reset "Reset")
*   Skills with the [Movement Label](/Movement_Label "Movement Label") (except [Berserk](/Berserk "Berserk"))
*   [Dodge](/Dodge "Dodge")
*   [Move](/Move "Move")
*   [Jump](/Jump "Jump")
*   [Climb](/Climb "Climb")

  

![sibylla-n5.png](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/sibylla-n5.png)

Sibylla's Advice

If the Fireteam Leader declares the [Berserk](/Berserk "Berserk") Skill, only the Fireteam members who have that Skill will be able to declare and perform it, the rest of the Troopers will declare and perform an [Idle](/Idle "Idle").

Example 6: Changing Leader, and Hacking (Fireteam in the Active Turn)
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Example 6: Changing Leader, and Hacking (Fireteam in the Active Turn)

![eng-fireteam-examples-4.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/eng-fireteam-examples-4.jpg)

During their Active Turn, the player with a Fireteam made up of five Fennec Fusiliers aims to carry out a [Comms Attack](/Comms_Attack "Comms Attack") against the enemy. To that end, during their new Fireteam Order, they decide that the Fennec Fusilier Hacker will be the Fireteam Leader and place the Fireteam Leader Token besides him.

Next, he declares the [Move](/Move "Move") Skill and the entire Fireteam advances. The Fireteam Leader declares the use of a [Hacking Program](/Hacking_Program "Hacking Program") against the enemy Trooper as the second Short Skill of the Order. In this case, the Fireteam Leader has not declared a [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack"), so no MODs for Fireteam Level Bonuses are applied. Additionally, the rest of the Fireteam members perform [Idles](/Idle "Idle"), so the Fireteam Leader is the only one to act. However, even if there is another Hacker in the Fireteam, they would not act and the Fireteam Leader would receive no [Bonus](/Fireteam_Bonuses "Fireteam Bonuses") whatsoever.

![eng-fireteam-examples-5.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/eng-fireteam-examples-5.jpg)

During the following Fireteam Order the first [Basic Short Skill](/Basic_Short_Skill "Basic Short Skill") of the Order is declared: Move. After the corresponding [Coherency](/Coherency "Coherency") Check and [ARO](/ARO "ARO") declaration, the second Short Skill of the Fireteam is declared: [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack"). Both the Fireteam Leader and the other Fireteam members Move, but only the Fireteam Leader performs a BS Attack Roll. Since this Fireteam is made up of 5 Troopers (Fennec Fusiliers), the Leader applies the [Level 5 Fireteam Bonuses](/Fireteam_Bonuses "Fireteam Bonuses"), so he gains BS Attack (+1 SD) Skill and a +1 MOD to his BS Attack.

Had [Discover](/Discover "Discover") been declared as the second Short Skill instead of a BS Attack, the Fireteam Leader would have been the only one to perform the [WIP](/WIP "WIP") Roll, receiving a +3 WIP MOD. In any of the previous cases, the other Fireteam members are vulnerable to enemy Trooper AROs—the opponent would make [Normal Rolls](/Normal_Roll "Normal Roll"), since the other members could not perform any Roll.

  

Example 7: Changing Leader and Fireteam Suffering a Casualty (Fireteam in the Active Turn)

Example 7: Changing Leader and Fireteam Suffering a Casualty (Fireteam in the Active Turn)

Now suppose that this Fireteam made up of 5 Troopers (Fennec Fusiliers) has suffered a casualty, who is in the [Unconscious State](/Unconscious_State "Unconscious State"). With a new Order, the player places the Fireteam Leader Token next to one of the Fireteam members, a Fennec Fusilier [Paramedic](/Paramedic "Paramedic"). Next, they declare the first Basic Short Skill: [Move](/Move "Move"). After this they perform a [Coherency](/Coherency "Coherency") Check, verifying that the other members fulfil the Coherency rule and are inside the new Fireteam Leader’s [ZoC](/ZoC "ZoC"). All of the Fireteam members are moved and the Fireteam Leader enters [Silhouette contact](/Silhouette_contact "Silhouette contact") with the Unconscious Fennec Fusilier.

During the second Short Skill, the Fireteam Leader uses their [MediKit](/MediKit "MediKit"). Meanwhile, the other Fireteam members are activated by the second Short Skill, but do nothing and perform an [Idle](/Idle "Idle") instead.

  

Example 8: Fireteam ARO (Fireteam in the Reactive Turn)
-------------------------------------------------------

Example 8: Fireteam ARO (Fireteam in the Reactive Turn)

![eng-fireteam-examples-6.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/eng-fireteam-examples-6.jpg)

As seen in the image, a Dāturazi armed with a Chain Rifle spends an Order during their Active Turn and declares a [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack") against the five- Zhanshi Fireteam. The Morat player verifies that the Chain Rifle’s Large Teardrop Template only affects three of the five Zhanshi (numbered 1, 2, and 3).

If the Yu Jing player declares [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack") as the [Fireteam ARO](/Fireteam_ARO "Fireteam ARO"), the three Zhanshi will each have to make a [Saving Roll](/Saving_Roll "Saving Roll"), so the player decides not to risk it. Instead, they declare [Dodge](/Dodge "Dodge") as the Fireteam ARO. Zhanshi 4, although unaffected by the Template, will use their Dodge to gain [Partial Cover](/Partial_Cover "Partial Cover"). Zhanshi 5, already in Partial Cover, declares [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack") as their [ARO](/ARO "ARO"). As Zhanshi 5 has declared a different Skill from the Fireteam ARO, they automatically stop being part of the Fireteam, lose all [Fireteam Level Bonuses](/Fireteam_Bonuses "Fireteam Bonuses"), and so their ARO only gets [Burst](/Burst "Burst") 1 and a +3 [Range](/Range "Range") MOD.

Suppose that, in the previous case, Zhanshi 1 to 4 declared BS Attack AROs and Zhanshi 5 a Dodge ARO. Zhanshi 5 automatically stops being part of the Fireteam and the Zhanshi that declare BS Attack only apply the Level 4 Fireteam Bonuses.

REMEMBER

If Zhanshi 5 is in [Coherency](/Coherency "Coherency") with the Fireteam Leader during the next [States Phase](/States_Phase "States Phase"), the player may apply the rule for rejoining a Fireteam and once again have a five-member Fireteam Core.

However, if the player is in their [Active Turn](/Active_Turn "Active Turn") and they want to have a five-member Fireteam Core without waiting, they will have to spend a [Command Token](/Command_Token "Command Token") and recreate the Fireteam following the [Fireteam Creation](/Fireteams:_Basic_Rules#Fireteam_Creation "Fireteams: Basic Rules") rules.

  

Example 9: Fireteam ARO 2 (Fireteam in the Reactive Turn)
---------------------------------------------------------

Example 9: Fireteam ARO 2 (Fireteam in the Reactive Turn)

![eng-fireteam-examples-7.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/eng-fireteam-examples-7.jpg)

During their Active Turn, an Arjuna and his Kiranbot declare [Move](/Move "Move") as the first [Basic Short Skill](/Basic_Short_Skill "Basic Short Skill") of the Order, in [LoF](/LoF "LoF") of the five-Zhanshi Fireteam. All five Zhanshi declare the same [ARO](/ARO "ARO"), [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack"), but three of them target the Arjuna while the other two target the Kiranbot.

Thanks to the [Peripheral (Synchronized)](/Peripheral_\(Synchronized\) "Peripheral (Synchronized)") Special Skill, the Arjuna and the Kiranbot can choose different targets. The Arjuna declares a BS Attack against Zhanshi 1 as the second Short Skill of the Order, and the Kiranbot also declares a BS Attack, but aimed at Zhanshi 2. As the Zhanshi form a Fireteam with 5 members belonging to the same Unit, all of them may apply the corresponding [Fireteam Level Bonuses](/Fireteam_Bonuses "Fireteam Bonuses").

A series of [Face to Face Rolls](/Face_to_Face_Roll "Face to Face Roll") ensues between the Arjuna, and Zhanshi 1 who has a +1 BS Attack MOD and BS Attack (+1 SD) Skill in ARO, so he rolls 2 dice and discards 1. Since the Kiranbot attacks with a [Direct Template Weapon](/Direct_Template_Weapon "Direct Template Weapon"), Zhanshi 2 makes a [Normal](/Normal "Normal") BS Roll, rolling 2 dice and discarding 1 thanks to his BS Attack (+1 SD) Skill in ARO, and applying a +1 MOD to his BS Attack. However, he will have to make a [Saving Roll](/Saving_Roll "Saving Roll") due to the Direct Template Weapon. The rest of the Zhanshi have not been attacked, so they also make Normal Rolls, applying the Fireteam Level Bonuses to their AROs.

  

Example 10: Fireteam in CC Combat vs. Martial Arts L5 (Fireteam in the Active Turn)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Example 10: Fireteam in CC Combat vs. Martial Arts L5 (Fireteam in the Active Turn)

A three-Zhanshi Fireteam Haris in their Active Turn declares a [Move](/Move "Move") + [CC Attack](/CC_Attack "CC Attack") against Miyamoto Mushashi, who has the [Martial Arts](/Martial_Arts "Martial Arts") L5 Special Skill. Only the Zhanshi designated as Fireteam Leader can perform the CC Attack, receiving a +1 B Bonus for each allied Trooper engaged in the Close Combat. Miyamoto can apply his Martial Arts Level 5, which grants him [B](/B "B")2 and (+1 SD), and targets the Zhanshi Fireteam Leader. A [Face to Face Roll](/Face_to_Face_Roll "Face to Face Roll") ensues between the Zhanshi’s B3 and Miyamoto Mushashi’s B2 with (+1 SD).

REMEMBER

Mushashi can choose any of the three Zhanshi as the target for his three dice, but he may only target one of them, since each Reactive Trooper can only target a single Fireteam Trooper (either the Fireteam Leader or any other Fireteam member), although it is not mandatory that all of them choose the same target.

Example 12: Fireteam Confrontation
----------------------------------

Example 12: Fireteam Confrontation

**Case 1: Fireteam Order (Move + BS Attack) against Fireteam ARO (BS Attack).**  
With a new Order, the Fireteam Leader of the five-Zhanshi Fireteam declares [Move](/Move "Move"), and the PanOceanian player declares [AROs](/ARO "ARO") during their Reactive Turn since two members of their five-Fennec Fusilier Fireteam gain [LoF](/LoF "LoF") to the Zhanshi. Both Fusiliers declare a [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack") ARO, but each of them can only target one of the Zhanshi that form the Fireteam. Fusilier 1 chooses Zhanshi 1 (Fireteam Leader armed with a Heavy Machine Gun) and Fusilier 2 chooses Zhanshi 2 (Combi Rifle). Both Fusiliers are armed with Combi Rifles and, since they are part of a five-member Fireteam, they gain all the [Bonuses](/Fireteam_Bonuses "Fireteam Bonuses") of a Level 5 Fireteam in ARO. In this case, both can apply the +1 MOD to BS Attack and BS Attack (+1 SD) Skill.

The Yu Jing player must now decide whether to Attack or [Dodge](/Dodge "Dodge"). Should they declare Dodge and win the [Face to Face Rolls](/Face_to_Face_Roll "Face to Face Roll"), they could avoid the BS Attacks against both Troopers, but this would not eliminate the threat. If they declare BS Attack, they will apply the Bonuses to the Fireteam Leader, but that would allow Fennec Fusilier 2 to make two [Normal Rolls](/Normal_Roll "Normal Roll") against Zhanshi 2, who would not be able to Dodge or perform a BS Attack, since they would be performing an [Idle](/Idle "Idle").

Finally, the player decides to risk it and declares BS Attack, applying the Level 5 Fireteam Bonuses (+1 BS Attack and BS Attack (+1 SD) Skill). The player decides to divide their Fireteam Leader’s [Burst](/Burst "Burst") 4 (Heavy Machine Gun B4 with (+ 1SD)) between the Fusiliers, with three Face to Face BS Attacks against Fusilier 1 and a Normal BS Attack Roll with one extra die thank to the (+1 SD) Bonus against Fusilier 2.

Thanks to the Bonuses provided for being part of a Level 5 Fireteam, Fusilier 2 can shoot in ARO with BS Attack (+1 SD) and with higher accuracy (+1 MOD to BS Attacks).

Both Normal Rolls against Zhanshi 2 are successful and the target, having failed a [Saving Roll](/Saving_Roll "Saving Roll"), enters the [Unconscious State](/Unconscious_State "Unconscious State").

Meanwhile, Zhanshi 1 wins the Face to Face Roll against Fusilier 1, who also becomes Unconscious after failing their Saving Roll. However, none of Zhanshi 1’s BS Attacks against Fusilier 2 are successful.

**Case 2: Fireteam Order (Move + BS Attack) against Fireteam ARO (Dodge).**  
Having lost one of their Zhanshi, the Yu Jing player only has the Level 4 Fireteam Bonuses now. Spending a new Order, they place the Fireteam Leader Token next to Zhanshi 3, who is armed with a Combi Rifle. The player declares [Move](/Move "Move") as the first [Basic Short Skill](/Basic_Short_Skill "Basic Short Skill"), but they only move the new Fireteam Leader, who gains [LoF](/LoF "LoF") to Fennec Fusilier 2. The PanOceanian player decides to declare [Dodge](/Dodge "Dodge"). The Fusilier Fireteam is now a Level 4 Fireteam, so they can only apply the corresponding [Bonuses](/Fireteam_Bonuses "Fireteam Bonuses"). Having declared Dodge, and thanks to their Fireteam Bonuses, the members of the Fireteam gain a +1 MOD to their Dodge Rolls.

Zhanshi 3 declares BS Attack against Fennec Fusilier 2. Now the players must make the appropriate Rolls to find out how this situation is resolved…

  

Example 13: Game Situations with Fireteams
------------------------------------------

Example 13: Game Situations with Fireteams

**Case 1: Fireteam Order, Changing Leader, and Movement (Jump).**  
During their next Order, the player chooses a new Fireteam Leader, placing the Fireteam Leader Token besides the Zhanshi armed with a Heavy Machine Gun. During this Order, since two of the Zhanshi are on a broken catwalk, the Fireteam Leader declares [Jump](/Jump "Jump") as the Fireteam Order. Next, a [Coherency](/Coherency "Coherency") Check is performed, which confirms that all the Fireteam members are still inside the new Fireteam Leader’s [ZoC](/ZoC "ZoC"). Therefore, all the Zhanshi can perform the Jump, moving horizontally even though they lack a gap to pass over, and the player indicates where each of them finishes their Jump. At the end of the Order, the player makes yet another Coherency Check.

**Case 2: Fireteam Order, Changing Leader, and carry out mission.**  
A Fennec Fusilier Fireteam Core is next to a Scenario’s Objective Console. The PanOceanian player spends an Order and changes the Fireteam Leader, placing the Fireteam Leader Token next to the Fennec Fusilier Hacker. As the Fireteam are out of range of any enemy Troopers, they declare [Move](/Move "Move") + Activate Console as the Fireteam Order. The Fennec Fusilier Hacker moves into [Silhouette contact](/Silhouette_contact "Silhouette contact") with the Console and makes the required Roll, without adding any Bonuses or MODs for being part of a Fireteam. Meanwhile, the rest of the Fusiliers also move for the first [Basic Short Skill](/Basic_Short_Skill "Basic Short Skill") of the Order, and they perform an [Idle](/Idle "Idle") for the second Short Skill.
