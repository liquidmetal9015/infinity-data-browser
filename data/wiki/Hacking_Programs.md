In Infinity, infowar and cyberwarfare tasks, defined as Hacking, are shouldered by the [Hackers](/Hacker "Hacker"), specialized Troopers who can use the different Hacking Programs which define quantronic combat.

Getting Started with Hacking

**Update** _PDF 5.2, October 2025_  
According to their nature, [Hacking Programs](/Hacking_Program "Hacking Program") can either be aggressive to affect enemy Troopers, defensive to protect Allied Troopers, supportive to boost Allied Troopers or affect scenery items and objectives in scenario missions.

**Original**  
According to their nature, [Hacking Programs](/Hacking_Program "Hacking Program") allow the Hacker to act in an aggressive or defensive manner, support other Troopers, interact with scenery, etc.

Upgrade Programs (UPGRADE)
--------------------------

Custom-made software tailored to the style and preference of specific infowar operatives. If available, they will be listed in the [Unit Profile](/Unit_Profile "Unit Profile"), in round brackets next to the name of the [Hacker](/Hacker "Hacker") or the [Device](/Hacking_Device "Hacking Device").

  

Supportware Programs
--------------------

These are programs of extended duration which are mainly designed to support [allied](/Allied "Allied") Troopers on the battlefield by granting them bonuses to improve their combat capabilities. Likewise, they may also be designed to hinder [enemy](/Enemy "Enemy") Troopers, applying negative MODs to them.

IMPORTANT

**SUPPORTWARE**

*   A Trooper can benefit from the effects of only one [Program](/Hacking_Program "Hacking Program") with the [Supportware Label](/Supportware_Label "Supportware Label") at a time.
*   Each [Hacker](/Hacker "Hacker") can only sustain one active Supportware Program at a time.
*   A Hacker can cancel an active Supportware Program by declaring another Program and expending the corresponding [Order](/Trooper_Activation "Trooper Activation") or [ARO](/ARO "ARO").
*   A Supportware Program is automatically cancelled if the target of the Supportware is targeted by a new Supportware Program, or if the Hacker running the Program enters [Isolated State](/Isolated_State "Isolated State") or any [Null](/Null "Null") States.

  

Example of Supportware Use and Cancellation

During their Active Turn, a Player that has two Troopers with [EVO Hacking Devices](/EVO_Hacking_Device "EVO Hacking Device") on the table, declares [Fairy Dust](/Fairy_Dust "Fairy Dust") with one of them and chooses [REMs](/REM "REM") as the [Troop Type](/Troop_Type "Troop Type") that will benefit from this Supportware.

For tactical reasons, they decide to spend the last Order on the other Trooper with an EVO Hacking Device to declare [Enhanced Reaction](/Enhanced_Reaction "Enhanced Reaction") on a REM located in the vanguard.

By designating a REM that was already benefitting from [Fairy Dust](/Fairy_Dust "Fairy Dust"), the new Supportware Program deactivates the Fairy Dust Program and the corresponding Token is removed from the table.

  

REMEMBER

A Supportware Program is automatically cancelled if the target of the Supportware is targeted by a new Supportware Program, or if the [Hacker](/Hacker "Hacker") running the Program enters [Isolated](/Isolated "Isolated") State or any [Null](/Null "Null") States.

  

Hacking Devices and Hacking Programs: Characteristics
-----------------------------------------------------

[Hacking Devices](/Hacking_Device "Hacking Device") and [Hacking Programs](/Hacking_Program "Hacking Program") have the following characteristics:

*   They act in the user's [Hacking Area](/Hacking_Area "Hacking Area").
*   They do not require [LoF](/LoF "LoF") to act, unless the Hacking Program's own description states otherwise.
*   Only Troopers in [Model](/Model "Model") form may be targeted by Hacking Programs.
*   They benefit, if applicable, from the target's [Targeted](/Targeted "Targeted") State.

  

Example of Quantronic Combat
----------------------------

Example of Quantronic Combat (Hacking)

**Step 1:**  
The Active Trooper declares a [Move](/Move "Move") from point A to point B.

The Reactive Trooper declares a [Carbonite](/Carbonite "Carbonite") Hacking Program [ARO](/ARO "ARO").

The Active Trooper declares [Reset](/Reset "Reset") for their second Skill of the Order. They do not declare a [BS Attack](/BS_Attack "BS Attack") against the Hacker because it is clear that they lack [LoF](/LoF "LoF") to the Hacker along their entire movement path.

**Step 2:**  
The [Face to Face Rolls](/Face_to_Face_Roll "Face to Face Roll") are performed.

Active Trooper, Reset ([WIP](/WIP "WIP") Roll, in this case there are no MODs to apply): 1 d20 with Success Value (SV) = 13.

Reactive Trooper, Carbonite (WIP Roll, in this case there are no MODs to apply): 1 d20 with SV = 13.

They both succeed, but the Reactive Trooper's 7 cancels the Active Trooper's 3.

![eng-hacking-example-1.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/eng-hacking-example-1.jpg)

**Step 3:**  
Therefore, as the Hacker wins the Face to Face Roll, the Active Trooper must perform 2 [Saving Rolls](/Saving_Roll "Saving Roll") due to [DA](/DA "DA") Ammunition, against PS 7. Saving Roll = 13 (PS 7 + BTS 6).

Due to the 14, the Active Trooper enters [Immobilized-B State](/Immobilized-B_State "Immobilized-B State"), placing an Immobilized-B Token next to them.

![eng-hacking-example-2.jpg](https://assets.corvusbelli.net/wiki/n5/eng-hacking-example-2.jpg)
