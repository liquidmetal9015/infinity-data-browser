CONTROLLED JUMP

SHORT SKILL / ARO

[Supportware, No Roll.](/Labels "Labels")

EFFECTS

*   When the user declares the use of this Program, which does not require a Roll, the Program's Effects are applied immediately, instead of in the [Resolution](/Resolution "Resolution") step, and a Controlled Jump Supportware Token (Sup: Controlled Jump) is placed beside the user.
*   If declared as an [ARO](/ARO "ARO") to an enemy Trooper declaring [Combat Jump](/Combat_Jump "Combat Jump"), this means that the -3 MOD will affect the enemy Trooper's [PH](/PH "PH") Roll.
*   It is not mandatory to declare Controlled Jump as an ARO to an enemy Trooper declaring Combat Jump, instead the Hacker can choose to wait and declare an ARO in the normal way.
*   As long as Controlled Jump is active, when performing the Combat Jump Skill every Allied Trooper applies a +3 MOD while every Enemy Trooper must apply a -3 MOD. If both players have Controlled Jump active, the effects of both Programs are cancelled.
*   A player cannot have more than one active Controlled Jump Program.
*   This Program's range covers the entire game table.

  

IMPORTANT

*   This Program affects Troopers even if they don’t have the [Hackable](/Hackable "Hackable") Characteristic.
*   Controlled Jump can be declared as an [ARO](/ARO "ARO") against a [Combat Jump](/Combat_Jump "Combat Jump") Long Skill anywhere on the table.

Short Skill / ARO

+3/-3 MOD to the PH of every Trooper that performs [Combat Jump](/Combat_Jump "Combat Jump").

  

IMPORTANT

**SUPPORTWARE**

*   A Trooper can benefit from the effects of only one [Program](/Hacking_Program "Hacking Program") with the [Supportware Label](/Supportware_Label "Supportware Label") at a time.
*   Each [Hacker](/Hacker "Hacker") can only sustain one active Supportware Program at a time.
*   A Hacker can cancel an active Supportware Program by declaring another Program and expending the corresponding [Order](/Trooper_Activation "Trooper Activation") or [ARO](/ARO "ARO").
*   A Supportware Program is automatically cancelled if the target of the Supportware is targeted by a new Supportware Program, or if the Hacker running the Program enters [Isolated State](/Isolated_State "Isolated State") or any [Null](/Null "Null") States.

  

Example of Supportware Use and Cancellation

During their Active Turn, a Player that has two Troopers with [EVO Hacking Devices](/EVO_Hacking_Device "EVO Hacking Device") on the table, declares [Fairy Dust](/Fairy_Dust "Fairy Dust") with one of them and chooses [REMs](/REM "REM") as the [Troop Type](/Troop_Type "Troop Type") that will benefit from this Supportware.

For tactical reasons, they decide to spend the last Order on the other Trooper with an EVO Hacking Device to declare [Enhanced Reaction](/Enhanced_Reaction "Enhanced Reaction") on a REM located in the vanguard.

By designating a REM that was already benefitting from [Fairy Dust](/Fairy_Dust "Fairy Dust"), the new Supportware Program deactivates the Fairy Dust Program and the corresponding Token is removed from the table.

  

REMEMBER

A Supportware Program is automatically cancelled if the target of the Supportware is targeted by a new Supportware Program, or if the [Hacker](/Hacker "Hacker") running the Program enters [Isolated](/Isolated "Isolated") State or any [Null](/Null "Null") States.
